Summary Budget:

Objectives / Justification / Business Need:

The TriForce Innovators project aims to create a dynamic, collaborative web-based portfolio prototype demonstrating our team's technical skills in web development, project management, and design. This initiative addresses the growing demand for comprehensive, visually appealing, and interactive online portfolios for professionals and teams in technology and creative industries.


Project Success Criteria:

1. The prototype functions seamlessly across all major web browsers.

2. The portfolio showcases all team contributions effectively.

3. Stakeholder feedback indicates satisfaction with usability, design, and functionality.

4. The project meets all deliverables and deadlines.


Assumptions and Constraints / Concerns:

Assumptions:

1. All team members will commit their required hours to complete assigned tasks.

2. Software tools and hosting services will remain functional without interruption.

3. Stakeholders will provide timely feedback on deliverables.


Constraints:

1. A tight schedule of 12 days to deliver all components.

2. A limited budget of $200.

3. Reliance on team members' availability and skill sets.


Concerns:

1. Potential delays due to unforeseen technical challenges.

1. Scope of Work:

The scope of this project is to design, develop, test, and deploy a web-based portfolio prototype. The portfolio will showcase the team's web development skills, documenting the steps taken throughout the project and demonstrating the capabilities of the final product. The scope includes:

- Planning and design of the user interface and user experience (UI/UX)

- Development of front-end and back-end systems

- Testing and QA to ensure functionality and usability

- Final adjustments, optimization, and deployment of the portfolio website


2. Location of Work:

The work will primarily be conducted remotely, with team members collaborating via online platforms such as MS Teams and GitHub. The physical location for collaboration is not restricted, but all deliverables will be developed and shared online through a centralized cloud platform (e.g., GitHub, Google Drive).

5. Applicable Standards:

- HTML/CSS: W3C standards for web development

- JavaScript: ES6 standards for modern JavaScript usage

- Security Standards: OWASP security guidelines for web applications

- Code Quality: Adherence to best practices for clean, readable, and maintainable code, including the use of comments and version control (Git)


6. Acceptance Criteria:

The final portfolio prototype will be considered complete and accepted based on the following criteria:

- Functional Requirements: All features outlined in the project plan (e.g., homepage, portfolio section, contact form) must be implemented and operational.

- UI/UX Standards: The portfolio must meet the agreed-upon wireframe and mockup designs, ensuring a clean, intuitive user experience.

- Performance: The portfolio should load within 3 seconds and be responsive across multiple devices (desktop, tablet, mobile).

- Testing & QA: The portfolio must pass user acceptance testing (UAT), including all specified unit and integration tests.


7. Special Requirements:

- Collaboration Tools: The team will use Slack for communication, GitHub for code versioning and sharing, and Google Drive for documentation sharing.

- Compliance: The project will adhere to web accessibility standards to ensure that the portfolio is usable by individuals with disabilities (WCAG 2.0).


8. Risk Management Plan:

Identified Risks:

- Technical Risk: Potential issues with integrating front-end and back-end systems.

- Time Management: Limited time for final adjustments could affect the final deliverable quality.

- Scope Creep: Uncontrolled changes to project scope could affect timelines.


Mitigation Strategies:

- Technical Risk: Early integration of components and regular testing during development.

- Time Management: Set clear priorities and realistic deadlines for each task.

- Scope Creep: Strict adherence to initial project scope, with any changes approved by the project manager.

Exercise Title:

Collaborative Code Sprint Challenge


Objective:

To improve teamwork, communication, and collaboration while reinforcing key project skills such as task delegation, efficient problem-solving, and adhering to project deadlines.


Activity Description:

Overview: The team will participate in a simulated coding challenge that mimics real project scenarios. The goal is to collaboratively build a small feature or fix an issue within a predefined time. The exercise emphasizes cross-functional collaboration and skill-sharing.


Preparation:

Timeframe: 2 hours

Tools Required: Laptops, internet connection, shared Git repository, communication tools (e.g., Slack, Zoom)

Pre-Defined Task: Create a responsive web page integrating a basic "Contact Us" form that captures user inputs and displays them in a styled summary section upon submission


Roles:

Name Role Responsibilities
Jacob Blenis Project Manager & Main Developer Oversees task delegation, ensures progress alignment, and writes core JavaScript functionality
Trey Burling HTML/CSS/Graphics Lead Designs the form layout and implements responsive CSS styles
Varun Raj Grandhi Integration & Testing Lead Integrates the form with JavaScript, performs testing, and reviews the code for issues

Execution Steps:

Phase Time Activities
Planning Phase 15 minutes Jacob assigns specific subtasks, ensuring all members understand their roles; Trey and Varun propose integration strategies
Development Phase 1 hour Each member works on their assigned task while maintaining open communication through a group call or chat
Integration Phase 30 minutes Varun integrates the form and scripts, ensuring functionality; all members test the feature and resolve issues collaboratively
Reflection Phase 15 minutes The team discusses what worked well and identifies areas for improvement in teamwork and communication

Expected Outcomes:

Enhanced Communication: Members will learn to communicate clearly and effectively under time constraints.

Improved Collaboration: The activity fosters respect for each member’s expertise and contributions.

Skill Sharing: Participants strengthen their understanding of each other's tasks and roles, fostering empathy and cross-disciplinary learning.

Team Cohesion: Reinforces the team’s ability to work together under pressure and enhances trust.


Debrief Questions:

1. Methodology:

This Risk Management Plan follows a proactive, systematic approach to identifying, assessing, mitigating, and monitoring risks. The methodology includes:

- Identifying risks based on brainstorming and past project data.

- Analyzing risks using qualitative and quantitative methods (risk probability and impact matrix).

- Developing mitigation strategies for high-priority risks.

- Monitoring risks through regular reviews and project updates.

- Documenting all risks in a centralized Risk Register for tracking.


2. Roles and Responsibilities:

Project Manager (Jacob Blenis): Oversees the risk management process, ensures adherence to the plan, and communicates with stakeholders.

UI/UX Lead (Trey Burling): Identifies and mitigates risks related to front-end design and visual components.

Integration Lead (Varun Raj Grandhi): Manages risks related to testing, backend integration, and deployment.

All Team Members: Actively identify risks during their tasks and communicate them to the Project Manager.


3. Budget and Schedule for Risk Management:

Budget Allocation: $500 for contingency (tools, additional resources, or time).

Schedule: Risk assessments conducted weekly during team meetings. High-priority risks are addressed immediately.


4. Risk Categories:

Technical: Issues with tools, code, or technology.

Schedule: Delays in deliverables or critical tasks.

Cost: Budget overruns due to unplanned expenses.

Resource: Availability or performance issues with team members or tools.

External: Risks outside the team’s control, such as stakeholder feedback or dependencies.
